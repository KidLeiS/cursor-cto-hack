# sushicode — Brand Guidelines

## The name

- Always **lowercase**: `sushicode`.
- One word — never "Sushi Code", "SushiCode", or "sushi-code".
- In sentence-initial position, keep it lowercase where the medium allows; if a
  capital is unavoidable, prefer restructuring the sentence.

## Tagline

> **sushicode is code.**

Short, declarative, lowercase. Use it as a hero line or a signature; don't
pad it with extra words.

## Voice & tone

- **Precise, calm, confident.** Like a good chef: minimal motion, high skill.
- Favor short declarative sentences. Cut filler.
- Technical but never cold — the craft metaphor (fresh, clean, deliberate)
  carries the warmth.
- Avoid hype words ("revolutionary", "game-changing") and emoji in product copy.

## Logo

Two lockups live in [`logo/`](logo/):

- **Mark** (`sushicode-mark.svg`) — a minimal nigiri glyph. Use for app icons,
  avatars, favicons, and tight spaces.
- **Wordmark** (`sushicode-wordmark.svg`) — mark + `sushicode` set in the
  display face. Use for headers, docs, and marketing.

### Clear space

Keep clear space around any logo equal to the height of the mark's "rice" base.
Nothing (text, edges, other logos) should intrude on that margin.

### Minimum size

- Mark: 20px tall minimum.
- Wordmark: 96px wide minimum (below this, use the mark alone).

## Typography

| Use | Typeface | Fallback |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Display / headings | Fraunces | "Times New Roman", serif |
| Body / UI | IBM Plex Sans | "Helvetica Neue", sans-serif |
| Code / mono | IBM Plex Mono | ui-monospace, monospace |

Headings use tight tracking (`letter-spacing: -0.03em`) and medium weight
(~560). Body stays regular weight for readability.

## Color

See [`palette.md`](palette.md). In one line: **wasabi green accent on a deep
nori-dark base**, with rice-toned text.

## Do / Don't

**Do**
- Keep the accent sparing and intentional.
- Pair the wordmark with generous negative space.
- Use semantic status colors for state.

**Don't**
- Recolor the logo outside the palette.
- Stretch, rotate, or add effects (shadows, gradients) to the logo.
- Capitalize or hyphenate the name.
- Set the accent as a full-page background.
